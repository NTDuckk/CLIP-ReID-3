Abstract
Vision-language models like CLIP have revolutionized person re-
identification (ReID) by enabling cross-modal semantic alignment.
However, most of the existing CLIP-based ReID methods suffer
from a critical limitation: semantic entanglement, where identity
and attribute features are indiscriminately compressed into a sin-
gle, undifferentiated token representation. This oversight fails to
account for their inherently distinct roles in characterizing individ-
uals. To address this limitation, we propose an Identity-Attribute-
Decoupled Tokenization (IADT) method, a hierarchical framework
with two synergistic components: Subject-oriented tokens that
model identity through a cross-modality feature inverse mapping
paradigm, preserving invariant biometric features; Attribute-aware
tokens that capture localized characteristics through the cross-
interaction of local features and learnable prototype vectors, dy-
namically focusing on discriminative regions without manual su-
pervision. The hierarchical tokenization enables disentangled yet
complementary representation learning: Identity and attribute se-
mantics are encoded into distinct embedding subspaces, while
cross-token contrastive learning establishes semantic reinforce-
ment through attention-guided feature interaction. Crucially, this
process does not require part-level annotations, making it directly
applicable to real-world deployment. Extensive experiments val-
idate effectiveness of the proposed method. For example, on the
Market-1501 dataset, IADT achieves 97.1% mAP (+2.5% over SOTA)
and 98.2% Rank-1 accuracy. For the challenging MSMT benchmark,
it attains 88.9% mAP (+1.7% improvement) with 93.1% Rank-1 accu-
racy, demonstrating consistent superiority. The code will be avail-
able at https://github.com/llraay/IADT.

Introduction
Person Re-identification (ReID), a cornerstone of intelligent surveil-
lance systems, addresses the critical task of retrieving individuals
across non-overlapping camera networks in real-world environ-
ments. As an integral component of public safety infrastructure,
ReID enables cross-camera tracking in large-scale surveillance sys-
tems deployed in transportation hubs, smart cities, and retail spaces.
Despite advances in deep learning, practical deployments remain
challenged by severe intra-class appearance variations induced by
illumination changes, viewpoint disparities, occlusions, and pose
diversity[5, 7, 15–17, 38]. These factors hinder model generalizabil-
ity, highlighting the need for robust feature learning frameworks
that balance discriminative ability with real-world complexity.
Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) [13] and Vision Trans-
formers (ViTs)[3, 8, 20] as the main backbone networks have driven
advancements in person re-identification. CNNs exploit inductive
biases and hierarchical convolutions to efficiently capture fine-
grained spatial patterns (e.g., clothing textures). In contrast, ViTs
leverage global self-attention to adaptively focus on discriminative
regions (e.g., occluded body parts) and mitigate viewpoint varia-
tions [27]. CNNs struggle with occlusions/pose shifts due to limited
receptive fields and redundant spatial partitioning, while ViTs sacri-
fice local details via patch tokenization and require extensive data to
avoid overfitting. These uni-modal trade-offs (discriminative detail
vs. holistic generalization) motivate multimodal approaches.

Recent advances in Vision-Language Pre-training (VLP) mod-
els, such as CLIP [21], have unlocked novel paradigms for person
re-identification. CLIP’s dual-encoder architecture, pre-trained on
massive image-text pairs, aligns visual and textual modalities into
a unified semantic space through contrastive optimization. This
enables cross-modal transfer of semantic knowledge (e.g., attribute
descriptions) without requiring additional supervision. Building
on this, CLIP-based ReID methods like CLIP-ReID [12] enhance
identity specificity by integrating learnable text prompts—trainable
vectors appended to identity-specific textual templates—to refine
visual-textual alignment as shown in Figure 1(a). TSLIP-ReID [24]
employs textual self-supervision to enhance identity-specific fea-
ture learning, while PromptSG [30] introduces pseudo-token learn-
ing to generate context-aware textual descriptions aligned with vi-
sual semantics as shown in Figure 1(b). Despite their advancements,
these methods inherit a critical flaw from CLIP’s architecture: se-
mantic entanglement, where identity and attributes are compressed
into homogeneous token representations. This conflation limits
discriminative ability in complex scenarios, such as distinguishing
individuals with similar attributes or resolving occlusions where
localized attributes dominate identity cues.
To address these limitations of existing CLIP-based ReID meth-
ods, we propose an Identity-Attribute-Decoupled Tokenization
(IADT) method that decouples identity and attribute semantics
using natural language-inspired descriptors. Unlike conventional
approaches that conflate these components into entangled embed-
dings, IADT aligns with human linguistic cognition by explicitly
separating identity-centric and attribute-aware semantics as shown
in Figure 1(c). Specifically, we introduce structured prompts: ‘a
photo of a 𝑆∗person with 𝐴1∗𝐴2∗· · ·𝐴𝑘∗’, in which 𝑆∗and 𝐴𝑘∗de-
note identity and attribute tokens, respectively. This design mir-
rors human descriptions by assigning identity and attribute infor-
mation to dedicated subspaces within the CLIP embedding space,
thereby effectively avoiding semantic interference. Then, we pro-
pose an instance-specific prompt learning framework that inte-
grates dynamic feature aggregation and context-aware filtering to
achieve cross-region semantic fusion, generating context-aware
fine-grained token embeddings. This framework enhances cross-
modal alignment by adaptively correlating localized visual features
with their textual counterparts while mitigating overfitting through
attribute-conditioned regularization and noise suppression. More-
over, a lightweight hierarchical network, which uses textual em-
beddings derived from the prompt as the query and patch-wise
embeddings from the visual encoder as the key and value, bridges
the inherent gap between linguistic and visual modalities. By syner-
gizing these components, IADT achieves deeper semantic mining:
attribute tokens refine local discriminability, while identity tokens
maintain global distinctiveness, overcoming the ‘discriminative vs.
holistic’ trade-off in uni-modal ReID.
Extensive experiments on four public benchmark datasets demon-
strate the superiority of our method. For example, on the Market-
1501 dataset, IADT achieves 97.1% mAP (+2.5% over SOTA) and
98.2% Rank-1 accuracy. For the challenging MSMT benchmark, it at-
tains 88.9% mAP (+1.7% improvement) with 93.1% Rank-1 accuracy,
demonstrating consistent superiority.
To summarize, the contributions of our work are as follows.
• We propose an Identity-Attribute-Decoupled Tokenization method
for person re-identification that decouples identity and attribute
semantics using natural language-inspired descriptors.
• An instance-specific prompt optimization framework based on
dynamic feature aggregation and context-aware filtering achieves
cross-region semantic integration, generating context-aware fine-
grained token embeddings.
• Prompt-based cross-modal learning utilizes language prompts
as a guide for image feature alignment, enabling the model to
localize regions corresponding to the textual descriptions.

Related Work
2.1
Person re-identification
Person re-identification has shown amazing promise in the field of
security and protection. Thanks to the capability demonstrated by
deep learning, recent work on person re-identification has opted
for Convolutional Neural Network[6, 28, 31, 36] and Vision Trans-
former based networks, both of which are the main backbone net-
works in today’s deep learning era. TransReID[8] is the first to
bring pure Transformer introduces ReID framework, extracts fea-
tures through the ViT backbone, and jointly optimizes ID loss and
triplet loss, which achieves state-of-the-art results. AAFormer[44]
introduces a part-level dynamic alignment mechanism within the
Transformer architecture, leveraging learnable part tokens to adap-
tively localize semantic human/object parts without manual annota-
tions. DC-Former[14] employs a multi-embedding space decoupling
strategy, decomposing the feature space into multiple compact sub-
spaces to enhance diversity. However, CNN-based and ViT-based
ReID methods primarily rely on visual single-modality and lack
the ability to integrate textual semantics, which limits the model’s
generalization understanding of cross-scene person attributes.

2.2
Vision-language models for ReID
Vision-Language pre-training Models (VLMs), capable of simulta-
neously processing image and text information, establish semantic
correlations between vision and language, demonstrating excep-
tional generalization abilities across many downstream tasks[1, 19].
CLIP (Contrastive Language-Image Pre-training), a landmark work
in VLMs, maps images and texts into a shared embedding space
through contrastive learning, achieving cross-modal semantic align-
ment. Inspired by recent advances in prompt learning from natu-
ral language processing (NLP)[9], CoOp[40] proposed a dynamic
prompt learning framework specifically designed for pretrained
models. Following this soft prompt approach, CLIP-ReID [12] pio-
neers the adaptation of CLIP for person ReID by classifying images
into ID specific prompts. Similarly, CSDN [32] introduces bimodal
learnable natural language descriptions to acquire the semantic
information corresponding to visual representations of visible and
infrared images. CCLNet [2] proposes a prompt learning paradigm
for unsupervised visible-infrared person re-identification by estab-
lishing a learnable cluster-aware prompt for person images and
obtain textual descriptions allowing for subsequent unsupervised
training. Differing from CLIP-ReID, Prompt-driven Semantic Guid-
ance (PromptSG) [30] focuses on vision-language alignment, whose
goal is to exploit rich semantic information from language to ex-
plicitly control the weights assigned to each patch or region, and
improve the two-stage framework by directly inverting images into
the language latent space. However, its reliance on simple reverse
network processing falls short of deeply capturing fine-grained hu-
man features. In contrast, our model employs a hierarchical design
that decouples fine-grained information, reduces interference from
heterogeneous semantic contexts, and highlights attribute-specific
regions through targeted attention mechanisms.
3
Methodology
In this section, we provide a comprehensive overview of our pro-
posed framework, as illustrated in Figure 2. Unlike existing methods
[30] that project images into coarse global pseudo-word tokens,
our approach introduces an Identity-Attribute-Decoupled Tokeniza-
tion by decomposing visual content into two distinct types: one
subject-oriented pseudo-word token and multiple attribute-aware
pseudo-word tokens. Specifically, we introduce Instance-specific
Prompt Learning framework that focuses on learning discrimi-
native attribute information embedded in spatially localized re-
gions of the image. By aggregating these region-specific features,
our framework generates attribute-aware tokens that capture nu-
anced visual details. Moreover, inspired by [30, 32], we introduce
a multi-modal patch-to-prompt interaction through a language-
guided cross-attention module that aggregates the attention map
to emphasize regions with high semantic relevance, allowing the
model to better align visual and textual information. This design
addresses the limitations of prior work by explicitly disentangling
holistic semantic concepts from localized attribute-specific details.
3.1
Instance-specific Prompt Learning
In conventional classification tasks, CLIP achieves strong perfor-
mance by mapping discrete class labels to semantically rich text
prompts, a process contingent on manually engineered prompt tem-
plates. In contrast, person ReID operates under an open-set recog-
nition framework, where identities are inherently unbounded and
lack predefined class labels. This fundamental discrepancy poses
a significant barrier: the absence of explicit semantic categories
renders conventional CLIP-style prompt engineering inapplicable,
as no textual descriptors exist to align visual features with identity-
specific semantics. To address this, we propose using visual features

4.1.2
Implementation Details. For fair comparison with previous

the Adam optimizer, setting the learning rate at 5e-6 for visual
encoders and 5e-5 for randomly initialized modules. A 10-epoch

to 5e-6. For CNN-based models, training spans 120 epochs with
learning rate decayed by 0.1 at epochs 40 and 70, while ViT-based
models train for 60 epochs with 0.1 decay every 20 epochs. We
set the number of selected attributes to 16 and 64 for learnable
queries in the attribute-aware pseudo-word tokens. As mentioned
earlier, we employ two three-layer MLPs (Multi-layer Perceptrons)
𝑓𝑆and 𝑓𝐴with 512-dimensional hidden layers to map the subject
and attribute tokens, respectively. Additionally, a three-layer single-
head Transformer is utilized to extract image attribute features. The
framework incorporates a Transformer-based dynamic attribute
feature aggregation module. In Eq. (9), hyper-parameter 𝜆is set to
0.1. All experiments are implemented using PyTorch. Comparative
studies with state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods on DukeMTMC and
Occluded-Duke datasets are conducted on a single NVIDIA 2080Ti

4.5
Pseudo-to-Real Description Retrieval
To validate the semantic alignment of our learned subject-oriented
𝑆∗and attribute-aware [𝐴1∗, ...,𝐴∗𝑘] pseudo-word tokens with natu-
ral language, we propose a cross-modal retrieval framework. Lever-
aging BLIP [11]), we construct a 10k-entry real-language attribute
corpus from pre-training data after filtering out the generic phrases
(e.g., “blurry photo”) on the Market1501 dataset. Each image is
encoded as a pseudo-text prompt (“a photo of a 𝑆∗person with
[𝐴1∗, ...,𝐴𝑘∗]”) within CLIP’s embedding space, and the most related
description from the top-20 results is retrieved via cosine similarity.
Considering the person re-identification task, all the subject descrip-
tions are ‘person’, therefore, we focus more on the retrieval perfor-
mance regarding the attribute descriptions. As illustrated in Figure
6, 𝑆∗tokens reliably map to person-centric contexts (e.g., “walking
down the street”), while [𝐴1∗, ...,𝐴𝑘∗] tokens capture discriminative
attributes (e.g., “striped shirt”, "holding a bag"). This demonstrates
that pseudo-tokens not only encode human-interpretable seman-
tics but also bridge the latent space between visual representations
and textual descriptions, a critical advancement for explainable
cross-modal ReID systems.

5
Conclusion
This paper proposes an effective framework called Identity-Attribute-
Decoupled Tokenization (IADT) for person re-identification tasks,
built upon the CLIP model. Unlike existing solutions, IADT in-
tegrates subject-oriented and attribute-aware tokens to address
the semantic entanglement limitation of current methods through
identity-attribute disentangled tokens. To capture fine-grained in-
formation and suppress background noise, we employ an instance-
specific prompt learning strategy combined with a local-global
relevance-based filtering mechanism. Furthermore, by designing
a structured text prompt template—“a photo of a 𝑆∗person with
𝐴1∗𝐴2∗· · ·𝐴𝑘∗”—we guide the model to focus on discriminative fea-
tures and achieve state-of-the-art recognition performance on four
challenging ReID datasets. This framework demonstrates the ef-
fectiveness of identity-attribute disentanglement and fine-grained
feature learning for robust cross-modal person re-identification.
